Varanasi, India

The Spiritual Capital of India

About Varanasi

Varanasi, also known as Benares or Kashi, is one of the world's oldest continually inhabited cities and a major pilgrimage site for Hindus. Situated on the banks of the sacred Ganges River, Varanasi is believed to be the earthly home of Lord Shiva.

For thousands of years, pilgrims have traveled to Varanasi to bathe in the Ganges, perform rituals, and seek spiritual liberation. The city's labyrinthine alleys, ancient temples, and vibrant ghats create an atmosphere unlike anywhere else on Earth.

Highlights of Our Varanasi Pilgrimage

  • Sunrise boat ride on the Ganges River
  • Evening Ganga Aarti ceremony at Dashashwamedh Ghat
  • Visit to Sarnath, where Buddha gave his first sermon
  • Exploration of ancient temples including Kashi Vishwanath
  • Yoga and meditation sessions with experienced guides
  • Interactions with local spiritual leaders and scholars

Sample Itinerary

Day 1: Arrival in Varanasi

Arrive at Varanasi airport, transfer to hotel, orientation session, and welcome dinner.

Day 2: Ganges Exploration

Sunrise boat ride on the Ganges, visit to various ghats, afternoon temple visits, evening Ganga Aarti ceremony.

Day 3: Sarnath Excursion

Visit to Sarnath, where Buddha delivered his first sermon, explore Buddhist monuments and museum.

Day 4: Spiritual Practices

Morning yoga and meditation, interactions with spiritual leaders, free time for personal reflection.

Day 5: Departure

Final morning rituals, farewell ceremony, transfer to airport for departure.
